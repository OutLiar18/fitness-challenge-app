# Champions Legacy

# Challenge Rules

Version: 2.0

---

# Purpose

This document defines the official rules governing Champions Legacy.

It serves as the single source of truth for:

- Players
- Team Captains
- League Administrators
- Platform Administrators
- Future Development

Whenever possible, application behaviour should reflect the rules defined within this document.

Where implementation differs temporarily due to development progress, this document takes precedence.

---

# Challenge Philosophy

Champions Legacy exists to encourage continuous personal growth through small, consistent improvements.

The challenge rewards:

- Consistency
- Discipline
- Honesty
- Healthy habits
- Continuous learning
- Personal improvement

The purpose of the challenge is not perfection.

The purpose is becoming better than yesterday.

---

# Core Principles

Every rule within Champions Legacy should support one or more of the following principles.

## Consistency

Regular effort is more valuable than occasional exceptional effort.

Small improvements performed consistently create meaningful long-term change.

---

## Fairness

Every participant should have the opportunity to compete regardless of age, experience or athletic ability.

Competition should reward commitment rather than natural talent alone.

---

## Honesty

The platform is built on trust.

Players are expected to record activities truthfully.

No automated system can replace personal integrity.

---

## Continuous Growth

Winning is rewarding.

Improving yourself is even more rewarding.

Every participant should finish the challenge stronger than when they started.

---

# Rule Hierarchy

When interpreting the rules, the following order applies.

1. Challenge Rules
2. League Rules (if applicable)
3. Administrator Decisions
4. Platform Behaviour

The application should enforce these rules wherever practical, however administrator judgement may occasionally be required.

---

# Participation

Participation is open to all eligible players invited to the challenge.

Players are expected to:

- Participate respectfully.
- Submit accurate entries.
- Follow the spirit of the challenge.
- Encourage fellow participants.
- Accept administrator decisions when necessary.

Participation is voluntary.

Players may withdraw at any time unless league-specific rules state otherwise.

---

# Sportsmanship

Champions Legacy promotes friendly competition.

Participants are encouraged to:

- Celebrate other players' success.
- Offer encouragement.
- Share knowledge.
- Help newcomers.
- Compete respectfully.

Winning should never come at the expense of integrity.

---

# Honesty Policy

Every submitted activity is considered a declaration that the player has completed that activity truthfully.

Dishonest submissions undermine the purpose of the challenge.

Examples include:

- Recording activities that were never completed.
- Deliberately exaggerating values.
- Recording duplicate activities.
- Exploiting known application bugs.
- Manipulating statistics.

Honesty is expected even when evidence is not required.

---

# Fair Play

Participants must never intentionally gain an unfair advantage.

Examples include:

- Falsifying entries.
- Exploiting software bugs.
- Circumventing challenge rules.
- Using another person's activity as their own.
- Manipulating devices or tracking applications.

Future versions of Champions Legacy may include automated fraud detection.

---

# Evidence

## Current Policy

Evidence requirements are determined by the challenge organisers.

Some leagues may require proof for selected categories.

Other leagues may rely entirely on the honesty policy.

The absence of required evidence does not remove the expectation of truthful participation.

---

## Acceptable Evidence

Depending on the activity, evidence may include:

- Photographs
- Screenshots
- Fitness tracker data
- GPS activity
- Reading reflections
- Administrator verification

Evidence requirements may vary between leagues.

---

## Future Expansion

Future versions of Champions Legacy may support:

- Automatic wearable integration
- GPS verification
- Image verification
- AI-assisted validation

These systems are intended to reduce administrator workload rather than replace trust.

---

# Daily Participation

Players may complete activities at any time during the day.

Activities may be split into multiple sessions unless a category specifies otherwise.

Example:

Morning:

- 20 Push-ups

Evening:

- 30 Pull-ups

Total:

50 Upper Body Repetitions

Daily goal achieved.

---

# Recording Activities

Entries should be submitted as soon as reasonably possible after completion.

Each entry should accurately reflect the activity performed.

Where multiple sessions occur throughout the day, players may submit multiple entries.

Historical entries may become locked after a configurable period depending on challenge settings.

# Daily Goals

Each category has a recommended daily goal.

Daily goals exist to encourage balanced personal development rather than maximise point accumulation.

Exceeding a daily goal is encouraged where appropriate.

However, exceeding a goal does not necessarily guarantee additional rewards.

Scoring systems may apply diminishing returns, effective repetitions or category-specific balancing.

The detailed scoring rules are documented separately in **POINTS_SYSTEM.md**.

---

# Relationship to Other Systems

This document defines the rules of participation.

It does **not** define:

- Point calculations
- XP calculations
- Achievement requirements
- League ranking algorithms
- Progression formulas

Those systems build upon these rules and are documented separately.

---

# Guiding Principle

When uncertainty exists, the interpretation that best supports honesty, fairness and long-term personal growth should always take precedence over competitive advantage.

# Category Rules

# Water 💧

## Daily Goal

**2,000 ml**

The daily goal is based on plain drinking water only.

The purpose of this category is to encourage proper hydration as part of a healthy lifestyle.

---

## Counts Towards Progress

Examples include:

- Still water
- Filtered water
- Bottled water
- Tap water
- Sparkling water (unsweetened)

---

## Does NOT Count

Examples include:

- Coffee
- Tea
- Milk
- Soft drinks
- Fruit juice
- Energy drinks
- Flavoured water containing sugar
- Alcohol

These beverages may contribute to hydration but do not count towards the Water category.

---

## Recording Water

Water may be recorded in multiple entries throughout the day.

Example:

Morning

500 ml

Afternoon

750 ml

Evening

750 ml

Total

2,000 ml

Daily Goal Complete ✅

---

## Notes

Players are encouraged to drink more than the daily goal where appropriate.

Additional water may contribute towards statistics, however rewards beyond the daily goal are determined by the Points System.

---

# Fruit 🍎

## Daily Goal

**3 Servings**

The purpose of this category is to encourage regular fruit consumption as part of a balanced diet.

---

## Counts Towards Progress

Examples include:

- Apple
- Banana
- Orange
- Pear
- Mango
- Pineapple
- Grapes
- Peach
- Plum
- Kiwi
- Watermelon
- Strawberries

This list is not exhaustive.

---

## Does NOT Count

Champions Legacy follows the **culinary definition** of fruit.

The following therefore do not count:

- Tomato
- Avocado
- Cucumber
- Pumpkin
- Butternut
- Courgette (Zucchini)
- Eggplant (Brinjal)
- Bell Pepper

Although botanically classified as fruits, these foods are treated as vegetables within the challenge.

---

## Serving Guidelines

One serving generally represents one medium portion of fruit.

Examples:

- 1 Apple
- 1 Banana
- 1 Orange
- A handful of grapes
- A cup of chopped fruit

Reasonable judgement should always be used.

---

## Maximum Daily Goal

The challenge goal is completed after three servings.

Additional fruit is encouraged but may not provide additional progression or rewards.

Refer to the Points System for scoring behaviour.

---

# Reading 📚

## Daily Goal

**30 Minutes**

The objective is to encourage meaningful learning rather than simply accumulating reading time.

---

## Counts Towards Progress

Examples include:

- Educational books
- Self-development books
- Academic textbooks
- Professional material
- Non-fiction
- Manuals
- Skill development books

Fiction may be permitted where challenge organisers choose to allow it.

---

## Does NOT Count

Examples include:

- Social media
- Random internet browsing
- Entertainment articles
- Comment sections
- Passive scrolling

The intention should always be deliberate reading.

---

## Reflection

Reading entries should include a short reflection or summary whenever required by the challenge organisers.

Reflection demonstrates understanding rather than simply recording time.

Future versions may support optional journal integration.

---

## Audiobooks

Current Rule

Audiobooks do not automatically qualify.

Individual leagues may decide whether active listening counts as reading.

---

## Re-reading Material

Re-reading valuable educational material is permitted.

Understanding and retention remain the primary objective.

---

# Daily Completion

A category is considered complete once its daily goal has been achieved.

Daily goals exist to encourage balanced development across all challenge categories rather than maximising performance in a single area.

Example

Water Goal

2,000 ml

Recorded

2,600 ml

Result

Daily Goal: ✅ Complete

Statistics Stored: 2,600 ml

Whether additional progress awards additional points is determined by the Points System.

---

# Recording Beyond Daily Goals

Players are encouraged to exceed daily goals where appropriate.

Additional activity may:

- Improve personal statistics.
- Contribute towards achievements.
- Count towards league objectives.
- Be recognised by future progression systems.

However, additional activity does not automatically guarantee additional points or rewards.

Scoring behaviour is defined separately within the Points System.

---

# Workout Philosophy

Workout categories measure overall training effort rather than individual exercises.

Players are encouraged to build balanced workouts using multiple movements.

Example

Upper Body

20 Push-ups

15 Pull-ups

15 Dips

Total

50 Effective Repetitions

Daily Goal Complete ✅

This approach allows players of different abilities to participate while encouraging exercise variety.

---

# Statistics

Statistics exist to measure personal progress over time.

Statistics should always record actual completed activity, even when additional rewards are not granted.

Examples include:

- Water consumed
- Distance run
- Reading time
- Exercise volume
- Cardio duration
- Skill practice time

Statistics should remain historically accurate and should never be altered by future balancing changes.

---

# Relationship to the Points System

Challenge Rules determine **what qualifies**.

The Points System determines **how qualifying activities are rewarded**.

This separation allows the platform to rebalance scoring without changing the rules of participation.

Examples of systems defined elsewhere include:

- Point values
- Difficulty multipliers
- Effective repetitions
- Bonus points
- XP
- Achievements
- League rankings

---

# Streaks

## Current Rule

Streaks are not yet active within the platform.

---

## Planned Behaviour

A streak represents consecutive days of successful participation.

Unless otherwise specified by league rules, a successful day is one in which at least one category reaches its daily goal.

Future versions may introduce:

- Grace days
- Freeze tokens
- Recovery mechanics
- League-specific streak rules

The complete implementation will be documented within the Progression System.

---

# Administrator Authority

Administrators are responsible for maintaining a fair and enjoyable challenge.

Responsibilities may include:

- Clarifying rules.
- Reviewing evidence.
- Resolving disputes.
- Correcting obvious mistakes.
- Enforcing fair play.
- Supporting participants.

Administrators should act consistently, transparently and in the best interests of the challenge.

---

# Appeals and Disputes

Participants may request clarification or review if they believe a decision has been made incorrectly.

Appeals should be:

- Respectful
- Honest
- Supported by evidence where possible

Administrator decisions should prioritise fairness, consistency and the spirit of the challenge.

Future versions may introduce formal appeal procedures.

---

# Rule Changes

Champions Legacy will continue to evolve.

When rules change:

- Historical entries remain valid.
- Completed seasons should retain the rules under which they were played.
- New rules should not unfairly invalidate previous achievements.

Major rule changes should be documented within the project history and release documentation.

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Can I split activities across the day?

Yes.

Multiple entries may be combined to achieve a daily goal unless a category specifically states otherwise.

---

## Can I exceed the daily goal?

Yes.

Additional activity is encouraged.

Whether it provides additional rewards depends on the Points System.

---

## Will extra progress still be recorded?

Yes.

Statistics should always reflect the activity actually completed.

---

## Can I combine multiple exercises?

Yes.

Workout categories measure total effective repetitions rather than individual exercises.

---

## What happens if I accidentally submit incorrect information?

Mistakes should be corrected as soon as reasonably possible.

League organisers may choose to allow corrections within a defined period.

Future platform features may automate this process.

---

## What happens if a rule is unclear?

The interpretation that best supports:

- Honesty
- Fairness
- Personal growth

should always take precedence.

Administrators may provide clarification where required.
