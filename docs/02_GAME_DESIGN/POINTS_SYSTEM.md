# Champions Legacy

# Points System

Version: 2.0

---

# Purpose

The Points System defines how qualifying activities are rewarded within Champions Legacy.

Its purpose is not simply to assign numbers.

Its purpose is to encourage long-term personal growth by rewarding consistency, effort and improvement while maintaining fair competition between players of different abilities.

The rules for what qualifies as an activity are defined in **CHALLENGE_RULES.md**.

This document defines how those qualifying activities are translated into points.

---

# Design Philosophy

A good scoring system should motivate players to improve themselves rather than exploit the system.

Champions Legacy rewards:

- Consistency
- Honest effort
- Personal improvement
- Balanced development
- Long-term participation

The system intentionally avoids rewarding raw athletic ability alone.

Someone who consistently improves from beginner to intermediate should feel just as successful as someone who is already highly advanced.

---

# Core Objectives

The Points System has six primary objectives.

## 1. Reward Consistency

Small efforts performed every day should outperform occasional extreme performances.

Daily participation is the foundation of Champions Legacy.

---

## 2. Reward Effort

More demanding activities should generally be worth more than easier alternatives.

However, no activity should become overwhelmingly superior.

---

## 3. Maintain Fair Competition

Players of different fitness levels should remain competitive.

The scoring system should minimise advantages gained solely through natural ability or prior experience.

---

## 4. Encourage Variety

Players should be encouraged to develop multiple aspects of health and personal growth rather than specialising in a single category.

---

## 5. Discourage Exploitation

The system should minimise opportunities to gain excessive points through repetitive, low-value activities.

---

## 6. Remain Flexible

The system should be capable of evolving over multiple seasons without requiring changes to the Challenge Rules.

---

# Relationship to Challenge Rules

Challenge Rules answer:

> "Does this activity qualify?"

The Points System answers:

> "How much should this qualifying activity be worth?"

Separating these responsibilities allows balancing changes without altering the rules of participation.

---

# Point Categories

Each challenge category awards points independently.

Current categories include:

- Water
- Fruit
- Reading
- Running
- Upper Body
- Lower Body
- Core
- Cardio
- Skill Development
- Steps

Additional categories may be introduced in future seasons.

---

# Base Points

Every category has a base point value representing the standard reward for meaningful progress.

Base values should remain relatively close together.

Large point differences between categories should generally be avoided.

This encourages players to pursue balanced growth rather than chasing whichever category awards the highest score.

---

# Category Balance

Different categories naturally require different levels of effort.

For example:

- Drinking water is easier than running 5 km.
- Reading for 30 minutes differs from completing a strength workout.
- Learning a new skill requires different effort than counting steps.

Rather than forcing every category to award identical points, Champions Legacy aims for **equivalent effort**, not identical actions.

Perfect equality is neither possible nor desirable.

Instead, the system seeks reasonable fairness across all categories.

---

# Effective Progress

Some activities are more demanding than others, even within the same category.

For example:

- Push-ups
- Bench Press
- Muscle-ups

all contribute towards Upper Body progress but require different levels of strength and skill.

To account for this, Champions Legacy uses an internal concept known as **Effective Progress**.

Effective Progress estimates the relative effort of an activity while remaining invisible to most players.

Its purpose is to maintain fairness rather than reward complexity.

---

# Difficulty Balancing

Certain exercises or activities may receive internal balancing adjustments.

These adjustments exist to recognise that:

- Some movements require greater strength.
- Some movements require greater skill.
- Some movements require greater endurance.
- Some movements require greater coordination.

Difficulty balancing should never create massive point gaps between players.

Its purpose is simply to recognise meaningful differences while preserving competitive fairness.

---

# Hidden Calculations

Not every calculation performed by the scoring engine is visible to players.

This is intentional.

Keeping certain balancing mechanics internal provides several benefits:

- Reduces unnecessary complexity.
- Discourages optimisation purely for points.
- Allows balancing adjustments between seasons.
- Encourages players to focus on improvement rather than mathematics.

Players should understand **why** they earned points without needing to know every formula used internally.

---

# Diminishing Returns

Champions Legacy encourages players to exceed their daily goals where appropriate.

However, extremely large amounts of activity should not dominate the leaderboard.

The Points System may therefore reduce the value of excessive progress beyond meaningful daily participation.

Examples include:

- Extremely high repetition counts.
- Excessively long reading sessions.
- Very large step totals.
- Marathon-length cardio sessions.

This allows extraordinary effort to remain rewarding without making consistent players uncompetitive.

---

# Daily Goals

Daily goals serve two purposes.

First, they provide achievable milestones.

Second, they establish the baseline for balancing the Points System.

Completing a daily goal should always feel meaningful.

Going beyond the goal may provide additional rewards, but the majority of value should come from consistent completion rather than occasional extremes.

---

# Consistency Over Volume

Champions Legacy intentionally rewards regular participation more heavily than isolated exceptional performances.

Example:

Player A

Completes every daily goal for seven consecutive days.

Player B

Completes an enormous workout once during the week.

Player A should generally receive greater overall rewards.

The challenge celebrates habits rather than isolated achievements.

---

# Anti-Exploitation

The scoring system should actively discourage behaviours such as:

- Repeating extremely easy activities purely for points.
- Splitting activities unnaturally to manipulate rewards.
- Recording duplicate entries.
- Exploiting balancing mechanics.
- Chasing points at the expense of genuine improvement.

Whenever an optimisation conflicts with the spirit of the challenge, the spirit of the challenge should prevail.

---

# Transparency

Players should always understand:

- Why points were awarded.
- Which category received points.
- When daily goals were achieved.

Players are **not** required to understand the underlying balancing calculations.

Transparency should improve trust without exposing unnecessary complexity.

---

# Seasonal Balancing

Point values may be adjusted between seasons.

Balancing changes should aim to improve fairness while preserving historical integrity.

Completed seasons should retain the scoring rules under which they were played.

Historical leaderboards should never be recalculated using new balancing rules.

---

# Administrator Guidelines

Administrators should avoid manually modifying points except where necessary to correct genuine errors.

Whenever possible:

- Calculations should be automated.
- Rules should be consistent.
- Adjustments should be documented.

---

# Future Expansion

Future versions of the Points System may include:

- Dynamic difficulty balancing.
- League-specific scoring modifiers.
- Seasonal bonus objectives.
- Team scoring.
- Personal best bonuses.
- Challenge streak bonuses.
- Adaptive balancing using analytics.
- AI-assisted balance recommendations.

These additions should complement the existing philosophy rather than replace it.

---

# Relationship to Other Systems

The Points System provides the foundation for several higher-level systems.

These include:

- Progression
- Achievements
- League Rankings
- Seasonal Competition
- Personal Statistics
- Milestones

Each system builds upon points while maintaining its own independent rules.

---

# Guiding Principles

Every future scoring change should satisfy the following questions.

Does this encourage consistency?

Does this reward genuine effort?

Does this discourage exploitation?

Does this keep beginners competitive?

Does this motivate long-term participation?

Does this encourage balanced personal growth?

If the answer to any of these questions is "No", the proposed change should be reconsidered.

---

# Changes Since Version 1.0

Version 2.0 introduces a comprehensive scoring philosophy rather than a simple list of point values.

Major additions include:

- Formal scoring objectives.
- Separation of rules and scoring.
- Effective Progress concept.
- Difficulty balancing philosophy.
- Hidden calculation framework.
- Diminishing Returns philosophy.
- Anti-exploitation principles.
- Seasonal balancing guidance.
- Future scalability for leagues and platform growth.

The original goal of rewarding healthy habits has been preserved while expanding the system into a flexible platform suitable for long-term development.
